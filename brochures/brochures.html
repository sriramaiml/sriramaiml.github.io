Our Products

Carefully designed, rigorously tested and refined over years

Our lifts are carefully designed and rigorously tested for safety, reliability, and performance. With modular components, they offer flexible solutions to fit any space or requirement. Whether for residential, commercial, or industrial use, our lifts are adaptable, ensuring seamless integration into your environment with top-tier functionality and ease.

Traction Model

Traction lifts use a system of ropes and a counterweight to move the lift car up and down smoothly. They are energy-efficient, reliable, and ideal for buildings of various heights.

These lifts provide a comfortable ride and are perfect for residential, commercial, or industrial spaces requiring safe and efficient vertical transport.

MRL Model

MRL (Machine Room-Less) lifts eliminate the need for a separate machine room, saving space and construction costs. They use energy-efficient motors mounted in the lift shaft.

Compared to traditional traction lifts, MRL lifts are more compact, easier to install, and maintain, making them ideal for buildings with limited space.

Hydraulic Model

Hydraulic lifts use a piston powered by fluid pressure to raise and lower the lift car, offering smooth and reliable operation. Recent advancements include eco-friendly hydraulic systems, energy-efficient designs, and improved ride comfort.

They are ideal for low to mid-rise buildings, offering flexibility in installation and space-saving options.

Goods Model

Goods lifts are essential for transporting heavy or bulky items efficiently in warehouses, factories, and commercial buildings. They enhance productivity, reduce manual labor, and ensure safety during material handling.

Investing in a goods lift streamlines operations, saves time, and minimizes the risk of injury, making them a vital asset for businesses requiring frequent vertical transport of goods.

Staircase Model

Staircase lifts provide safe and easy mobility for individuals with knee pain or limited mobility, making them indispensable in hospitals and elderly homes.

They reduce the risk of accidents on stairs, promote independence for patients, and ensure caregivers can focus on care rather than physical assistance, creating a safer and more accessible environment.
